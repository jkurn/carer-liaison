name: Headlines must come from lived experience
description: Don't write headlines from frameworks or competitor research — they fail the "mum test" every time
type: feedback
---

Never write headlines or key copy from positioning frameworks, competitor research, or marketing theory. They will fail.

**Why:** In a single session, 5+ headline iterations were rejected. Each was technically sound (NDIS operational gap, invisible work, pattern recognition) but none landed. Jonathan said they all sounded like "marketing to his mum" rather than something that would make her stop and feel seen. The breakthrough came from office hours — the emotional core ("it takes a village," "mise en place") emerged from Jonathan describing his family's lived reality, not from any framework.

**How to apply:**
- Start from the specific, human experience — not the category or market gap
- The gold standard is mum's own words (still pending — the assignment is to watch her use the journal)
- Test copy against: "Would a 60-year-old carer who's been doing this for 28 years feel seen by this?" If it sounds like a pitch deck, rewrite it
- Frameworks are useful for structure (sections, flow, belief shifts) but NOT for the emotional hook
- When in doubt, use Jonathan's raw descriptions of his family's experience as source material — they are more compelling than any copywriting technique
