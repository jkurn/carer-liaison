**Wedge product:** AI carer journal (demo at `/demo2/`). Journal first, platform vision second. The journal is the right entry point because: zero setup (just talk about your day), validates the carer, captures knowledge that lives only in their head, and generates data that powers everything else later.

**Emotional core (discovered in office hours 2026-03-27):**
- "It takes a village" — carers need to feel someone is alongside them in the journey, not rescued
- "Mise en place" — the good day feeling is everything in its right place so the day flows instead of fights
- This is the product vision: mise en place for caring

**Agents = "Todone" list:**
- Agents should NOT be framed as more tasks or automation
- They are a "todone" list — things that have been handled FOR you
- The distinction: a to-do list adds weight, a todone list removes it
- Examples: NDIS evidence compiled, therapist briefed, meal plan adjusted

**Landing page structure (as of 2026-03-27):**
Hero → The Reality → The Journal → What It Gives Back → The Vision (todone) → NDIS → CTA

**Why:** The previous approach (leading with NDIS operational gap, invisible work framing) produced headlines that sounded like marketing. The emotional core comes from Jonathan's lived experience, not positioning frameworks.

**How to apply:** Always lead with the journal as the entry point. Frame agents as things handled, not things to do. Copy should make carers feel accompanied, not rescued. The "mise en place" vision should guide feature prioritization — does this make the day flow?
